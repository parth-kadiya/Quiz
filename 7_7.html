Diabetic dyslipidaemia with CKD & statin intolerance

4. Why is fenofibrate not preferred in this patient with CKD stage 3?
Please select an option first
Correct answer: B) May worsen renal function and cause further kidney function deterioration.
Rationale: Fenofibrate is renally excreted and can accumulate in CKD stage 3, increasing the risk of nephrotoxicity. It is not recommended in patients with eGFR <60 mL/min/1.73 m².
